This is the website for the 2017 NASSP masters Fundamentals of Radio Interferometry course. The course is based on the ipython-based book of the same name. This site will be updated regularly.

Homework Questions

The last slide of every lecture contains a homework question. You are required to hand in the answer of the homework question of your previous lecture at the beginning of your next lecture. It is crucial to attempt the homework as the hand written exam will consist of similar questions. Only the best five homework submission will count for credit.

Assignments

There are four official assignments, only your the best two will count for credit.

  • Assignment 1: Fourier Transform (due March 13)
  • Assignment 2: Positional Astronomy and Visibility Space Problem Set (due March 20)
  • Assignment 2: An implementation of Clark's CLEAN Method (due April 10)
  • Assignment 3: An implementation of StEfCal (due April 24)

Exam

The exam will consist of two parts:

  1. CASA exam: The CASA exam project will entail reducing a real KAT-7 measurement set and writing up the steps followed as well as the results in a report. Students will also be given a 15 min time-slot to present their results.
  2. Written exam: The written exam will mostly be based on the homework questions and the assignments.
